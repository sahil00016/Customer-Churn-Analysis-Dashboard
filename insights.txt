Summary
The analysis evaluates customer churn patterns to identify risk segments and retention opportunities.

Major Findings
- Customers with short tenure show significantly higher churn probability.
- Month-to-month contracts experience the highest churn.
- Higher monthly charges correlate with increased churn likelihood.
- Certain internet service categories show elevated churn rates.

Behavior Patterns
- New customers are most vulnerable to churn.
- Long-term contract customers are more stable.
- Customers paying higher charges without additional services show increased risk.

Business Implications
- Early engagement programs should be implemented for new customers.
- Long-term contract incentives can improve retention.
- Pricing adjustments may reduce churn among high-cost segments.
- Service bundles could reduce churn probability.

Conclusion
Customer churn is strongly influenced by contract type, tenure, and pricing. Businesses can significantly reduce churn by targeting high-risk segments with tailored retention strategies.
